Traditional Injera Recipes from Ethiopia: Fluffy, Sourdough Flatbread with Unique Flavor and Texture.

Injera is a leavened flat bread. It is high in carbohydrate specifically starch which is a source of heat and energy. A good injera should be soft, glossy, fluffy & rolable with an even distribution of “eyes”(a honey comb like porous structure) on the top surface.

Ingredients

  • teff flour 500 g
  • Water 1450 ml
  • Ersho 25 ml

Utensils

Bowl, sieve, sefed, sauce pan, ladle, mitad, and mesob

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Sift teff PM flour in a bowl.
  2. Mix the flour with 450 ml water and knead well for about five minutes.
  3. Put the dough in a container with a lid.
  4. Add ersho on top of the dough and cover.
  5. Allow the dough to ferment for 24 hrs.
  6. Boil 500 ml water in a saucepan.
  7. Mix part of the fermented dough (200 g) with 75 ml of cold water.
  8. Prepare absit by add the mix in the boiling water and cook for about 2 min by constantly stirring
  9. Cool absit to about 45ᵒC and add to the fermenting dough.
  10. Add 250 ml of water, mix and allow to ferment for 2-3 hrs at room temperature.
  11. Heat mitad and polish with rapeseed powder using a piece of clean cotton material.
  12. about 500 ml of the batter in a circular manner on heated mitad and bake covered for about two minutes.
  13. Remove the injera from the mitad by sliding over sefed.
  14. Serve with sauces (vegetable or pulse or meat).

Meaning of new Amharic word

Teff
refers to a small grain native to northern Africa, particularly Ethiopia and Eritrea, which is cultivated for its edible seeds and used to make flour
Ersho
Batter left from previous fermentation (starter culture).
Sefed
Traditional straw mat used for winnowing.
Mitad
Clay griddle used to bake injera.
Mesob
refers to a traditional Ethiopian and Eritrean basket used for storing injera, a flatbread.
